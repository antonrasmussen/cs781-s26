Diabetes Prediction Report - Anton Rasmussen

1) Model + Preprocessing

I used Logistic Regression as the final model after comparing it against Random Forest. The data came from the Pima Indians Diabetes dataset, with Outcome as the binary target. Preprocessing was handled in a pipeline to avoid leakage: an 80/20 stratified train/test split, median imputation for missing values (after treating physiologically impossible zeros as missing in Glucose, BloodPressure, SkinThickness, Insulin, and BMI), and standard scaling for logistic regression. I also used stratified 5-fold cross-validation during model selection.

2) Confusion Matrix

The matrix shows 75 true negatives, 38 true positives, 25 false positives, and 16 false negatives. False positives are patients predicted diabetic who are not diabetic, which can lead to extra follow-up testing. False negatives are diabetic patients predicted non-diabetic, which is generally the riskier error in screening because cases may be missed.

3) SHAP / Shapley Value Plot

The strongest drivers were Glucose, BMI, Pregnancies, DiabetesPedigreeFunction, and Age. This ranking is medically plausible: glucose is central to diabetes diagnosis, BMI is linked to insulin resistance, and age/family-history-related effects are expected risk factors. Lower SHAP impact for Insulin and SkinThickness is plausible here because those columns had substantial missingness in this dataset.
